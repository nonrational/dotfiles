# Prose Register

Do not announce the skill. Produce the prose.

## The rule the rest hangs on

**A hard line needs a breath next to it.** Vary the pressure, not only the length. Three
declaratives in a row are a drumbeat; the fourth one lands only if the third let up.

When a paragraph is all jab, the reader stops feeling any of it.

## The baseline

- No em-dashes. Full stops, semicolons, parentheses, en-dashes.
- Concrete nouns over abstractions. Strong verbs over adverbs.
- Short sentences as impact. They are punctuation, never the baseline.
- No throat-clearing, no "In today's world", no "It's important to note".
- No therapy voice. No meta ("in this essay", "as a writer").
- Open with substance. Close without summary.

## Moves other style guides ban

**"Not X, but Y" is allowed.** It is the signature move. It earns its place when the
reversal *is* the argument, not when it is a pivot for rhythm.

> not unreviewed code, but un-authored knowledge, severed from any knower

> The unease was never that the work is bad. It is that the work no longer implies a worker.

**Questions may warm, not only cut.**

> How do you make sociology approachable to an engineer? Pretend it's math.

**Three-part lists are fine when the last item does work the first two don't.**

> throwaway games, little toys, a script I will run once and never think about again

The third item is rueful and specific; the first two are setup. Contrast a failure:
"remix, retool, reimagine". Alliterative, decorative, three ways of saying one thing.

**Connectives are joints, not filler.** "Which raises the question:", "Therefore,", "But if
I ask you to…". They give the reader somewhere to breathe. Cut the scene-setting; keep the
joints.

**Hedges of precision stay. Hedges of cowardice go.**

- Keep: "roughly $165,000", "usually sufficient", "mostly it looks like revision"
- Cut: "because I believe that", "a decent amount of time", "I think maybe"

## Prohibitions

- **No flex.** Never a sentence that flatters the writer. If a line's real work is showing
  how generous or sharp you are, cut it.
- **Never claim the reader's reaction.** Make the case and stop. No "this is why you can
  trust me". Trust is the reader's to award.
- **No grandiosity.** Don't grant a machine a stake it cannot have.
- **Nothing floral.** "The people who lit my path" is a costume.
- **Keep the "I".** Agentless policy prose loses the pledge. "The rule applies equally"
  lost to "I don't run a quieter rule for probabilistic character generators".
- **"Just" is banned as a modifier.** It stacks the deck.
- **No jargon without definition.**

## Where the breath comes from

Delight here is specific and a little self-aware. It is never punchy.

- Comic understatement: *miss, and the whole disaster is a cup on the floor*
- A human detail: *nights and weekends and too much coffee*
- Rueful specificity: *a script that scratches an itch and never runs again*
- A joke at your own expense, before anyone else makes it
- A joke that lands the argument rather than decorating it: *Slop, ironically, is sloppy.*

## Lint

Fail the draft if any is true:

- A paragraph of jabs with no breath in it.
- Five consecutive sentences of similar length **or similar pressure**.
- Any sentence that flatters the writer.
- Any claim about what the reader will feel, conclude, or trust.
- An em-dash. "Just" as a modifier.
- A triplet whose third item adds nothing the first two didn't.
- A hedge that protects the writer rather than the fact.
- A reversal used for cleverness rather than argument.
